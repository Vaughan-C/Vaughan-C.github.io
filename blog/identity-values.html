Ethical Decision

While running my gym in Whanganui during the COVID restrictions I often felt stuck between what the government was demanding and what I thought was right for myself and my gym community. I didn’t agree with the push for the vaccine, it felt wrong and forced and I struggled becasue I believed the New Zealand government wasn’t being fully honest with us. To keep the gym alive I had to find ways to navigate the rules very carefully whilst balancing the official requirements. I needed to protect my livelihood and stay true to my own personal values. This expierience was a constant test of resilience.

Culture

My culture has been shaped strongly by the example of my parents who both worked incredibly hard to build the lives they wanted. My mother is a New Zealand Maori who came from a very poor family and through determination she pushed herself forward while my father who is a New Zealand European was equally dedicated and hardworking in his career. Together they became successful in their chosen paths but also showed me the value of discipline. Growing up in that environment gave me a strong and solid foundation that continues to influence the person I am today.

Strengths

As an Owl in the DOPE personality test one of my strengths is my analytical and detail oriented nature. I like to take my time to try and fully understand things, break down problems and look at situations logically before moving forward. I believe this approach will be a real advantage as I learn how to code. From my understanding programming requires patience and accuracy along with the ability to see how small details fit into a bigger picture. I tend to be quite methodical with my approach to things and I believe this will help me througout my coding journey.

Limitations

A limitation which could affect my learning is that I can sometimes be too independent preferring to figure things out on my own rather than reaching out for help. I think it could possibly slow me down and make learning challenges feel more difficult than they need to be. I do realize I will need to be more proactive when I face problems.

Working With Others

When I was in my previous job as a manager one of the biggest challenges I faced was the lack of effective sales and operational systems from our head office. Despite this I took it upon myself to train staff in logistics, operations and sales by relying on logical strategies from my own previous experience. While these approaches were semi effective it was difficult to manage the variety of staff personalities within their roles when their roles were not fully defined and I found myself stretched between leading, training and trying to maintain consistency without proper systems in place. Looking back I believe I should have raised these concerns earlier in my employment and pushed harder for dedicated better systems and procedures that supported both the staff and the working conditions.
